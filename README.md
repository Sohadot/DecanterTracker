DecanterTracker is not a wine site, a tasting app, or a cellar tracker. It is a **governed reference asset**: a domain built into the defining reference layer for a category that does not yet have one — the **accountable record of the moment a high-value bottle leaves custody and enters service.**

Every wine system is strongest *before* the cork: ownership, storage, inventory, valuation, tasting opinion, provenance. They fall silent at the moment the bottle is opened. DecanterTracker governs precisely that moment — the single irreversible service event that no existing system documents with discipline.

This repository holds the asset's public reference surface and its governing standard. It is intentionally structured so that a serious reader — an operator, a partner, or an acquirer — can understand the category, the standard, and the boundary in minutes.

## I · The methodology behind this asset

DecanterTracker is produced under the **Sovereign Asset System** — a method for building domains into reference-grade digital properties rather than trading them as strings of characters. The discipline is what creates the value, and it is visible throughout this asset.

**Doctrine first.** The asset begins from a single governing sentence, not from features. Everything downstream — the standard, the language, the page — is derived from it. *A great bottle is made over years and opened in a moment. Its record should honor both.*

**A defined category, not a crowded market.** DecanterTracker does not compete on cellar tracking, ratings, or reviews. It defines and governs a category those systems leave empty: **the opening record**. Owning the category is more defensible than competing inside one.

**A published standard.** The asset is anchored by `DT-ORS-001`, a public reference standard with seven clauses (see [`OPENING_RECORD_STANDARD.md`](./OPENING_RECORD_STANDARD.md)). The value is in *defining* the standard, not in selling a template.

**A disciplined boundary.** The asset attests; it does not adjudicate. It will not become an insurer, an appraiser, an authenticator, or a court. This restraint is deliberate — it is what makes the record trustworthy and the asset legally clean. *(This is the Artifact–Subject Separation principle: describe the event; never perform the verdict on it.)*

**Income must extend authority, never contaminate it.** The asset is not monetized through low-grade affiliate clutter or template sales. Its value path runs through partnership, pilot, licensing, or acquisition with parties who understand what owning a reference layer means.

The result is an asset that reads as institutional infrastructure — which is exactly what a strategic buyer or operating partner is qualifying when they arrive here.

## II · The standard — `DT-ORS-001`

A complete opening record answers, without interpretation:

> **What** bottle, in **what condition**, opened by **whom**, under **what authority**, **before whom**, **how served**, with **what incident if any**, reaching **what end.**

Nothing in that sentence is a verdict. Every element is an observation. That is the whole discipline.

**The seven clauses**

| # | Clause | Governs |
|---|--------|---------|
| 1 | The Opening Authority | Who held permission to open the bottle, and on what basis |
| 2 | Pre-Opening Condition | Fill, closure, label, sediment, provenance on file — described, never graded |
| 3 | The Witness Principle | Before whom the bottle was opened |
| 4 | The Service Record | Time opened, decanted, poured, served, to whom |
| 5 | Fault & Incident Posture | Corked, oxidized, declined, wrong bottle — stated as observed, never decided as fault |
| 6 | Consumption Closure | Consumed, partial, returned, lost — the event ends visibly, not by silence |
| 7 | Record Integrity | Made at the time, attributed, amended only visibly, self-contained |

**The custody boundary**

| The record holds | The record withholds |
|------------------|----------------------|
| What was opened, in what condition, by whom, before whom, how served, what incident, what end | Who was at fault, whether a guest was right, whether provenance is authentic, what the bottle is worth, whether a remedy is owed |

**Record completeness model** — `R0` Unrecorded → `R1` Self-declared → `R2` Condition-logged → `R3` Witnessed → `R4` Authorized service record → `R5` Institutional attestation. The model reads *completeness*, never truthfulness, value, or liability.

Full text: [`OPENING_RECORD_STANDARD.md`](./OPENING_RECORD_STANDARD.md).
